This is X's
Moving Manifesto.

This is for those who are unassured of their positions in the world; the population which identifies with X. X is interconnected but confused. Their cultures, beliefs, and modes of communication blend into each other. X is displaced. X is in transit. To belong is a privilege. X is born of fragmented communities—unable to identify with a specific one. There is an enduring need for the reorientation, reorganization, and reinvention of the self. The voice of tradition is large and permanent; there is no space for the undefined. The voice of the privileged is aggressive and unaccommodating, seeking to reconfigure their world. X makes a sound and goes unheard. Who speaks for X if they are neither A nor B? Who speaks for X if they’re both A and B? A population certain only of its uncertainty whispers through subversive channels. It collects nuance and metaphors, rally signs that won’t agitate. X is always circumventing power structures set in place by the command of an assertive singularity—pushing through schemas that try to write it out of history. How many whispers will amount to a shout? X exists in a state of continuous change, revising its paradigm as it traverses age and location. It reconstructs its axioms and recognizes that perspective evolves with experience.
